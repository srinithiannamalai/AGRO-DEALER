Cultivation Process of mango tree

    Cultivation of mango tree

    1. Selecting a Suitable Location

    Mango trees require plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil.

    2. Preparing the Planting Site

    Clear the area of any weeds, rocks, or debris. Dig a hole about twice as wide and deep as the root ball of the mango tree.

    3. Planting the Mango Tree

    Remove the mango tree from its container carefully, place it in the planting hole, backfill with soil, and water thoroughly.

    4. Watering and Mulching

    Keep the soil consistently moist and apply a layer of organic mulch around the base of the tree.

    5. Fertilizing

    Fertilize the mango tree regularly during the growing season, using a balanced fertilizer.

    6. Pruning and Training

    Prune the mango tree annually to remove dead or diseased branches and train it to a desired shape.

    7. Pest and Disease Management

    Monitor the tree for pests and diseases and control them promptly using appropriate methods.

    8. Harvesting

    Mango trees typically start bearing fruit 3 to 5 years after planting, depending on the variety.

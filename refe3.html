Cultivation Process of groundnut

    Cultivation of groundnut

    Process of Groundnut Cultivation

    1. Land Preparation

    Clear the land of any weeds and debris. Plow and harrow the soil to a fine tilth.

    2. Seed Selection and Treatment

    Select healthy, disease-free seeds. Treat the seeds with fungicides to prevent seed-borne diseases.

    3. Sowing

    Sow the treated seeds in well-prepared rows or ridges at the recommended spacing and depth.

    4. Irrigation

    Provide sufficient water to the groundnut crop, especially during the flowering and pod formation stages.

    5. Weed Control

    Keep the field weed-free through regular manual weeding or use of herbicides.

    6. Fertilization

    Apply fertilizers containing nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium according to soil test recommendations.

    7. Disease and Pest Management

    Monitor the crop regularly for signs of diseases and pests such as leaf spot, rust, aphids, and termites. Control them using appropriate measures.

    8. Harvesting

    Harvest the groundnut pods when they are fully mature. Pull the entire plant or use a mechanical harvester. Dry the pods in the sun for several days.

    9. Post-Harvest Handling

    After drying, remove the pods from the plants. Clean and grade the pods based on size and quality. Store them in a cool, dry place.

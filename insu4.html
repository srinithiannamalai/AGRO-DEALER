National Crop Insurance Program :

The National Crop Insurance Program (NCIP) is a federal initiative in the United States aimed at providing crop insurance coverage to farmers against losses due to natural disasters, adverse weather conditions, and other unavoidable risks. Administered by the Risk Management Agency (RMA) under the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), NCIP offers various insurance policies tailored to different crops and regions. Participating farmers pay a premium, which is subsidized by the government to make coverage more affordable. The program plays a crucial role in stabilizing farm incomes, protecting agricultural lenders, and ensuring the nation's food security.
